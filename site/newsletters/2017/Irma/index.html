Hurricane Irma…Be Prepared
 
 
 
 
 
 
Hurricane Irma’s impacts will start being felt this Saturday throughout Florida. As with any serious storm, your personal safety and that of your family should always be your primary concern. Many of our customers also ask what they can do to safeguard their electronics during storms such as this, so we wanted to send a quick tip list to make sure you do everything you can to prepare. 
 
 
  • Power down all non-critical electronics and unplug them from the walls (in the event of a power surge).

  • Move any important equipment away from windowed rooms, elevate them off the floor and cover with plastic if possible (only when powered off and unplugged).

  • Move all mobile (MacBooks, iPads, etc.) devices and secure them.

  • Backup any important files to either a cloud-based backup solution (Google Drive - https://www.google.com/drive/ or DropBox - https://www.dropbox.com  or an external hard drive (to be kept in a waterproof enclosure).

  • GatorTec always recommends keeping complete cloud-based backups of your computer using software such as Carbonite - https://www.carbonite.com or BackBlaze - https://www.backblaze.com

  • In the event of power loss, we strongly recommend plugging devices back into battery backup systems (APC), so in the event of inconsistent power, no damage is done to the electronics.

  • Be aware of apps such as Zello that claim offer walkie-talkie functionality between phones. These apps still require cellular service or wifi connectivity, which may not be available for sometime after a storm.

  • Battery cases or power banks are great options to keep iPhones and iPads fully charged during potential power outages.
